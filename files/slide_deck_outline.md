**Speaker Notes:**
"Another common problem: you type in your details, click Log In, and Instagram says 'Your username or password is incorrect.' Frustrating, right? But there are a few simple things to check.

First, look at your keyboard. Is the Caps Lock key on? Passwords are case-sensitive, which means 'Password' and 'password' are considered different. If Caps Lock is on, turn it off and try again.

Second, check your spelling carefully. It's easy to accidentally add an extra letter or miss one out. 

Third, check for spaces. Sometimes we accidentally press the space bar before or after typing. There shouldn't be any spaces before or after your username or password.

Here's a great tip: click that eye icon I showed you earlier. This lets you see what you're actually typing, so you can spot any mistakes. Much easier than trying to guess!

If you've checked all of these and it's still not working, it's time to use the 'Forgot Password' option we just covered."

- Example code shown: "123 456"

**Speaker Notes:**
"Sometimes, Instagram will ask you for a security code after you've entered your password. You might think 'Oh no, what's wrong?' But actually, this is a good thing! It means Instagram is protecting your account.

Instagram sends you a security code when you log in from a new device or a new location. It's Instagram's way of checking that it's really you trying to log in, and not someone else who has guessed your password.

Here's what to do: Check your phone for a text message, or check your email inbox. You'll receive a 6-digit code - it'll look something like '123 456.' Type this code carefully into Instagram, then click 'Confirm.'

That's it! You'll be logged in, and Instagram now knows this device is safe for you to use. You usually won't need to do this again on the same device.

This feature is called 'two-factor authentication,' and it's one of the best ways to keep your account secure. Some people find it a bit annoying, but it's worth it for the extra protection!"

- Each tip in a different color box for visual interest

**Speaker Notes:**
"Before we finish, let me share five crucial safety tips. These will help keep your Instagram account secure.

Tip number one, and this is the most important: Never, ever share your password with anyone. Not your friends, not your family, not even someone claiming to be from Instagram. Your password is like the key to your house - you wouldn't give that to a stranger!

Tip two: Use a strong password. Mix up letters, numbers, and symbols. Instead of 'password123,' try something like 'BlueCats2025!' - it's much harder for someone to guess.

Tip three: If you're using a shared computer - like at a library or a friend's house - always remember to log out when you're finished. This stops the next person who uses that computer from accessing your account.

Tip four: Be very suspicious of emails asking for your Instagram password. Instagram will never, ever ask for your password by email. If you get an email like this, it's a scam - delete it immediately.

And tip five: Trust your gut. If something feels suspicious or too good to be true, it probably is. If someone you don't know is asking for personal information, don't give it to them. Stay safe out there!"

## DESIGN GUIDELINES FOR ALL SLIDES:

**Colors:**
- Use high contrast combinations
- Suggested palette:
  - Primary: Deep blue (#1E3A8A)
  - Secondary: Bright teal (#0891B2)
  - Accent: Warm orange (#F97316)
  - Background: White or very light gray (#F9FAFB)
  - Text: Dark gray (#1F2937)

**Typography:**
- Headings: Bold, 36-44pt
- Body text: 24-28pt (minimum)
- Never go below 20pt
- Use sans-serif fonts (Arial, Helvetica, Calibri)
- Maximum 2 font families per presentation

**Layout:**
- Generous white space
- Maximum 3-4 bullet points per slide
- Maximum 6-7 words per bullet point
- Left-align text (easier to read)
- Use images and icons liberally

**Accessibility:**
- Describe visual elements verbally
- Don't rely solely on color to convey meaning
- Provide alt text for all images
- Test with grayscale filter to ensure contrast

**Consistency:**
- Same layout template throughout
- Consistent use of colors
- Icons in same style
- Slide numbers in footer

## PRESENTATION DELIVERY TIPS:

**Body Language:**
- Smile naturally
- Make "eye contact" with camera
- Use hand gestures to emphasize points
- Sit up straight, but relaxed
- Show enthusiasm without being over-the-top

**Voice:**
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Pause between main points
- Vary your tone to maintain interest
- Emphasize key words
- Don't rush, even if nervous

**Technical:**
- Test screen sharing beforehand
- Have backup plan if demo fails
- Keep glass of water nearby
- Minimize notifications
- Close unnecessary tabs/programs

**Timing:**
- Practice until you can do it in 9-10 minutes naturally
- Have a watch/timer visible to yourself
- Know which sections you can condense if running over
- Know which backup slides you can add if running under

**Engagement:**
- Use phrases like "Let me show you..." and "Here's what you do..."
- Speak conversationally, not like reading a script
- Acknowledge that this might feel overwhelming at first
- Show empathy for beginners' anxiety
- Express confidence that they can do this
